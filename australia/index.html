Step by Step

How to Apply — Visa Process

From choosing a course to landing in Australia — the full journey for Nepali students.

1
Choose Course & University
Research programs, compare universities and confirm CRICOS registration. Check entry requirements for Nepali qualifications.
2
Give IELTS / PTE
Most universities require IELTS 6.0–6.5 overall. Book your test 2–3 months before your intended application date.
3
Apply to University & Get CoE
Submit your application with transcripts, SOP, and IELTS scores. Upon acceptance you receive a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) — essential for visa.
4
Get OSHC Health Insurance
Purchase Overseas Student Health Cover before applying for visa. Many universities offer this directly.
5
Apply for Student Visa (Subclass 500)
Apply online via ImmiAccount. Upload CoE, OSHC, financial evidence, police clearance, and health checkup documents. Pay AUD 710 fee.
6
Medical & Biometrics
Book medical examination through BUPAS Nepal. Biometrics may be required at Australian visa application centre.
7
Visa Granted — Fly to Australia!
Processing takes 4–12 weeks. Once granted, you can enter Australia up to 90 days before course start date.

Important Tips

Before You Apply — Must Know

⚠️
Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)
Australian visa officers assess your intention to return home. Your SOP must clearly show ties to Nepal and why you plan to return after studies.
💰
Show Strong Financial Evidence
Bank statements should show funds for at least 1 year of study + living. Fixed deposits and property documents also help.
📊
Academic Gap Explanation
Any gap between your studies must be explained with genuine reasons. Unexplained gaps increase visa rejection chances.
🚨
Beware of Fake Consultancies
Always verify your consultancy is in the government registered list. Never pay more than Rs. 50,000 as service fee without written receipt.
📅
Apply at Least 3 Months Early
Visa processing can take up to 12 weeks. Start gathering documents as soon as you receive your CoE.
🏠
Sort Accommodation Before Leaving
Book at least first 2 weeks of accommodation before departure. University accommodation or homestay is safest for first arrival.
