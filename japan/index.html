🌸 Why Japan is Great for Nepali Students

Japan offers one of the lowest tuition costs among developed countries. Part-time work (convenience stores, restaurants, factories) pays JPY 1,000–1,500/hr (~Rs. 900–1,350/hr). A typical student can earn JPY 80,000–120,000/month to cover most living costs. Japan is also extremely safe and has a growing demand for foreign workers post-graduation.

Step by Step

Japan Student Visa Process

1
Learn Japanese (or find English program)
Start Japanese classes in Nepal. JLPT N5/N4 is enough for language schools. Some universities offer English-taught programs requiring IELTS/TOEFL.
2
Apply to Japanese Institution
Apply to a Japanese language school or university. They handle the immigration application on your behalf in Japan.
3
School Applies for CoE
Your school applies to Japan Immigration for a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE). This takes 2-3 months. The school sends it to you in Nepal.
4
Apply for Visa at Japan Embassy
Take CoE to Japan Embassy Kathmandu. Apply for student visa. Processing is 5-7 days. Fee is minimal.
5
Get "Permission to Engage" Stamp
At Japanese immigration on arrival, request stamp to work part-time (28 hrs/week). Without this you cannot legally work.
6
Start School & Find Part-Time Work
Convenience stores (conbini), restaurants, and factories are most common jobs for Nepali students. Pay is JPY 1,000-1,500/hr.
